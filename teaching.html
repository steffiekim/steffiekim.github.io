Teaching Philosophy

I believe that successful learning involves three essential elements: (1) a safe learning environment, (2) access to hands-on, embodied activities, and (3) curiosity-directed motivation. My role as the instructor is to assist each student in discovering and developing their potential while being able to collaborate with others. In doing so, I must have a kind heart to think from students' standpoints to understand their challenges and expectations. At the same time, I should also abide by fundamental rules to avoid probable harm to the learning environment. As the instructor, I try my best to find the right balance between reason and emotion. Similarly, I enjoy combining theories and practical, hands-on approach in my class that can activate the learning experience. Ultimately, I want my students to hone critical thinking skills that can guide their decision-making in life.
