Project #2

#2. The Influence of Group Play
On In-Game Generosity

This is a research project I led as a first author in collaboration with thatgamecompany. The high-level objective of the study is to investigate how to reduce toxicity among players in a mobile MMOG, Sky: Children of the Light.

The Problem

Massively multiplayer online games (MMOGs) feature social activities and generate in-game friendships. However, social settings can also bring about contagious toxic behavior (e.g., cyberbullying, harassment, griefing). Previous literature suggests that teamwork yields prosocial outcomes. To mitigate unhealthy in-game behavior, it is essential to investigate whether group play can encourage players to be generous, and if it is beneficial for both the community and the players.

The Goal

Conduct quantitative research to discover (a) group play creates in-game generosity and (b) whether players benefit from being generous.

Research Questions

1. Can group play increase generosity in-game?

2. Do players benefit from being generous?

Research Design

• A survey questionnaire was distributed in Sky: Children of the Light in November 2020
• The survey data was linked to six months of in-game player log data (April to October in 2020)
• A total of 1,056 unique players analyzed

Eligibility Criteria
• 18 years and older (M = 23.02, SD = 5.12)
• Active for at least 30 days during the previous 6 months

Analysis

• Path analysis was conducted in R using the lavaan package
• A residualized covariance matrix was created by regressing all variables in the model on the control variables (for methods, see Shah et al., 2007) in advance to control for the variance accounted for by covariates (e.g., age, gender, playtime)
• The residualized covariance was used to compute the path estimates

Key Findings

Generosity among players increased when:
• Players engaged in more group play than solo play

Group players have more benefits than solo players
• They gain more emotional support from other players
• They gain more information from other players
• They are more popular

Generous players have more benefits than less generous players
• They gain more information from other players
• They are more popular

Research Impact

Provides insights on how to reduce in-game toxicity
• Group play causes a player to be popular because they become generous
• Group play will make the in-game community a place where users want to stay due to increased number of generous players (can expect decreased churn rate)
• Designers/moderators should encourage group behavior and generosity among players

Achievements

• The findings of this research project were presented at the 107th National Communication Association conference

• The suggestions of this research project were delivered to thatgamecompany

• A first-authored manuscript is under preparation for publication
