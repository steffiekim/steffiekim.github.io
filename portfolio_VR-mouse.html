Mouse-Tracking Gender Effects
in Virtual Social Scenarios

This is a quantitative research project I conducted for my dissertation at USC.

Project

The Problem

Socially anxious individuals frequently face conflicting desires in social situations, where they may simultaneously desire social interaction and solitude. Despite this, there is still a lack of understanding regarding their behavior and reaction in real-time social scenarios. Research has indicated that social anxiety disorder can manifest differently in men and women and can evolve differently over time. Men and women, in general, engage in socializing differently, but does that also applies to socially anxious individuals? There is a need for further investigation into the complex nature of social anxiety disorder and how it affects individuals differently.

Research Questions

How does gender affect internal conflicts in virtual social scenarios?

The Goal

Investigate how women and men react differently to virtual social scenarios.

Research Design

  • 200 socially anxious individuals were recruited on Prolific.
  • For the purpose of quantatitive data analysis, participants who identified as neither male nor female were excluded.

    • Designing virtual social scenarios:
  • In-depth interviews were conducted with 27 socially anxious individuals to design virtual social scenarios.
  • 3 virtual social scenarios (party, grocery, job interview) were made using the Sims 4.

    • The experiment:
  • Prior to participating in the main experiment, individuals were given the opportunity to familiarize themselves with decision-making tasks through non-social trials (e.g., selecting an image of broccoli).
  • In the main experiment, participants were asked to choose either an approach or an avoidance choice in three different social scenarios.
  • For each virtual social scenario (party, grocery, job interview), participants encountered 5 decision-making situations, resulting in a total of 15 decision-making tasks throughout the experiment.

    • An example of a virtual social scenario (grocery)

    Analysis

  • After data cleaning, 176 observations (128 women and 48 men) were included in the analysis.
  • To account for the violation of normal distribution, robust independent-samples t-tests and robust one-way ANOVAs were performed in R.

    • Key Findings

  • Compared to socially anxious men, socially anxious women were more likely to move the mouse cursor directly to the choice, less likely to change the decision, and took less time to make a social decision in general, regardless of whether the choice was approach or avoidance.
  • Socially anxious men seem to experience more internal conflicts in imminent social scenarios, compared to socially anxious women.
  • Women are more likely to use defensive pessimism, a coping strategy for anxious individuals to think through possible outcomes in anticipating worst-case scenarios before social events. This can lead to lower ambiguity and internal conflict when making social decisions for socially anxious women.

    • Recommendations

  • Interventions for socially anxious individuals should be customized to gender-specific needs.
  • Gender-specific safety policies should be implemented for social interactions in VR environments.

    • Impact

  • The first study to employ mouse-tracking technology to track in-the-moment internal conflicts of socially anxious individuals in virtual social situations.
  • Highlighted the importance of gender in the development of VR interventions for socially anxious individuals.
  • Verified that virtual social scenarios can induce social anxiety.

    • Unanswered Questions

  • Develop a more comprehensive model that incorporates various gender identities (e.g., non-binary).
  • More proportionate participants for each comparison group supported by additional funding will increase the power of analysis.
  • The findings should be confirmed in immersive VR headsets for better application.
  • Examine if defensive pessimism is positive or negative for socially anxious individuals in social situations.
