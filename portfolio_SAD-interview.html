Mental Health Research Project

This is a research project I conducted as a Lead Mixed-methods Researcher at USC SOLVE-IT

Project

Communication Strategies of Socially Anxious Individuals

To develop an effective communication skills intervention for socially anxious individuals, I conducted formative research to find out their coping strategies and communication patterns.

Photo created by wayhomestudio at Freepik

The Problem

Individuals with social anxiety disorder (iSAD) experience day-to-day hardship, such as lower educational and work achievement, compared to their healthy counterparts. iSAD are prone to social isolation, even though they desire intimate interpersonal relationships. To develop an effective intervention for iSAD, it is critical to understand when they experience social anxiety, what factors determine their social anxiety, and how they handle it.

Research Questions

When do iSAD experience social anxiety?
What factors determine the level of social anxiety?
What are iSAD's communication strategies to manage social anxiety?

The Goal

  • Find out daily social situations in which iSAD experience social anxiety.
  • Investigate communication patterns iSAD use to overcome social anxiety.


    • Research Design

  • Interviewed 30 individuals with social anxiety disorder from Januaray to April in 2019.
  • An hour-long semi-structured interview for each participant (View interview protocolView interview protocol).

    • Participant Eligibility Criteria
  • Between the ages of 18 and 22 (M = 20.41, SD = 2.95).
  • Have a clinical level of social anxiety disorder based on the DSM-5 diagnostic criteria and the Liebowitz Social Anxiety Scale.


    • Analysis

  • Transcribed the interviews.
  • Analyzed using thematic analysis in NVivo.


    • Key Findings

    iSAD experience social anxiety when:
  • Their goals and the other party's goals are different
  • There is a mismatch in the common background (e.g., from a different city)
  • Their self-image is perceived differently (both positively and negatively) than their expectation

    • Factors that determine the level of social anxiety:
  • Benefits of socializing (e.g., What makes socializing better than staying at home?)
  • Communication competency (e.g., Can I present myself as a fun person?)
  • The amiability of the other party (e.g., Is this person friendly enough?)

    • iSAD's communication strategies to manage social anxiety:
  • Avoid socializing actively (e.g., do not attend the event) or passively (e.g., go on the phone at the party)
  • Pursue social interactions when inevitable, but plan ahead or collect information about other people
  • Put on a different persona (e.g., assuming a character from a TV show)


    • Key Findings

  • iSAD have the need to belong, but dislike social interactions. They desire socializing, but they do not have adequate social skills.
  • Basic social communication skills may not be naturally learned for iSAD. Social skills training is necessary for iSAD.


    • Recommendations

  • Clinicians can train iSAD to practice how to respond to a range of typical scripted social situations to achieve mutually rewarding social interactions.
  • Interventionists can train iSAD by initiating social interactions with friendly partners at the beginning stage and gradually decreasing the conversation partnerâ€™s level of affability over time.


    • Lessons

  • It would be helpful to have a note-taker for interviewees who do not want to be recorded. iSAD are sensitive populations with a high level of anxiety. Therefore, recording may not be an option for the researcher to revisit after the interview.
