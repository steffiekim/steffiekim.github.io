This is a personal project to showcase my dashabord skills.

Project

Note

Please be aware that this project is purely hypothetical and has been developed to showcase my dashboarding skills using publicly available data. With unrestricted access to data, I've crafted more intricate visualizations and conducted advanced data analytics. Unfortunately, due to confidentiality constraints, I am unable to share them online. Please consider this example as a brief glimpse into my capabilities.

The Problem

As a pioneering VR company, Blast introduced the virtual K-pop boy band Plave just nine months ago (at the time of analysis). The recent growth in their fanbase has made it challenging to pinpoint the current target audience. Given the unprecedented nature of a virtual boy band, with Blast leading the way in this innovative endeavor, comprehensive data analysis becomes essential. As Plave gears up to launch another album, understanding their fanbase is crucial for effective promotion on social media.

Questions

Who constitutes Plave's fan base?
When was the search term "Plave" the most popular over the months?

The Goal

  • Identify key characteristics of Plave's fanbase.
  • Explore strategies to enhance search engagement based on historical patterns.

    • Data Collection & Preparation

  • Retrieved search popularity history data from the largest Korean search engine, Naver, spanning from Plave's debut to the period preceding the release of their new album.
  • Segmented the data by gender, device, and age group for comprehensive analysis using Python.
  • Created an interactive data visualization dashboard using Tableau (See above).

    • Key Findings

    Gender:
  • Given that Plave is a boy band, it's evident that females show a greater interest in searching for Plave.
  • However, the male demographic is not negligible, with only a 3.2 difference.

    • Device:
  • PC users displayed a higher level of search interest for Plave compared to mobile users in general.

    • Age Group:
  • Individuals expressing interest in Plave predominantly fall within their teens and 20s (23% aged 19-24, 21.3% aged 13-18). Remarkably, Plave's appeal extends across a diverse age spectrum, even reaching those over 60.
  • Upon segmenting the age group data by gender, the highest engagement comes from the 19-24 age group for both female (32.7) and male (17.9) users. However, an intriguing shift occurs when considering the second-highest score: for male users, it is in the 55-59 age group, while for female users, it is in the 13-18 age group.
  • When segmenting by device, in comparison to mobile users, PC users exhibit higher engagement scores among age groups 30 and above.

    • Other Observations:
  • The highest 3 peaks are after album releases.

    • Overall:
  • While there are ups and downs in-between, the overall search popularity has increased over time. Plave is getting more popular.

    • Recommendations

  • For PC, target those over 30 as they are more active on PC compared to mobile.
  • Do not target women exclusively as men are also instested in Plave.

    • Limitations

  • Given the reliance on public data for this visualization, the range of analyses was constrained by the limitations imposed by the way Naver manipulated the original data.
  • Gender classification is restricted to two categories, which could be enhanced to be more gender-inclusive.
  • If I had access to the raw data, I could run t-test on gender differences to see if the differences are not due to mere chance.
